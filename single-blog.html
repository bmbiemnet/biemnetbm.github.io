Describing Your Daily Routine

By BM Insights • Published July 2026

One of the very first things you will be asked when making friends or speaking with a language partner is, "What do you do every day?"

To confidently describe your day-to-day life in Tigrinya, you need two main tools in your vocabulary toolkit: Time Expressions and Daily Activity Verbs. Let's break them down so you can start talking about your schedule immediately.

1. Essential Time Expressions

Before you can say what you do, you need to say when you do it. Here are the three most important time markers to structure your routine:

Time of Day Tigrinya Script Transliteration Listen
Morning ንጉሆ N'guho
Afternoon ድሕሪ ቐትሪ D'hri qetri
Evening / Night ምሸት M'shet

2. Common Daily Activity Verbs

Now that we have our timeline, let's plug in the action. Remember, when you are talking about your own routine, you will generally be conjugating these verbs in the first-person singular ("I" form).

Action (I do...) Tigrinya Script Transliteration Listen
I wake up ይትስእ y't's'e
I eat breakfast ቁርሲ ይበልዕ qursi y'bel'e
I go to work ናብ ስራሕ ይኸይድ nab s'rah y'kheyd
I sleep ይድቅስ y'dq's

Putting It Together

You can instantly build a simple story of your day by combining these two tables. For example, if you want to say "In the morning, I wake up," you simply combine N'guho and y't's'e.

Practice writing out your own timeline using these words, and don't forget to book a 1-on-1 session to practice speaking your routine out loud!
